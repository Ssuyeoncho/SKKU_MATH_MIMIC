In particular, mathematicians have long been interested in what kinds of hyperbolic metric structures can be given to a space, and what can be said when we group together hyperbolic metric structures that are similar in an appropriate sense. In a space equipped with a hyperbolic metric, we first study the possible isometric motions. We then introduce the mapping class group, which is obtained by grouping the self-homeomorphisms of a surface into isotopy classes. After that, we consider the Teichmüller space, which consists of hyperbolic metric structures on a surface modulo an appropriate notion of isotopy. Finally, we introduce the moduli space, obtained by identifying elements of the Teichmüller space that become equivalent under the action of the mapping class group. prerequisite_label_en: "Prerequisite" prerequisite_en: "Topology, Abstract Algebra I" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://youtu.be/ce9uqV8TSIo" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2025Fall/thumbnails/11_Introducing Mapping Class Group, Teichmuller Space and Moduli Space.jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Introducing Mapping Class Group, Teichmuller Space and Moduli Space" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Introducing Mapping Class Group, Teichmuller Space and Moduli Space" url: "/seminars/2025Fall/slidedecks/11_Introducing Mapping Class Group, Teichmuller Space and Moduli Space.pdf" ---
