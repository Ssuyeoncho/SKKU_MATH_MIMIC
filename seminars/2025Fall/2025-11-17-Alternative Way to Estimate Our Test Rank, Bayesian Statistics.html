When predicting what certain statistics (such as the distribution of scores, variance, standard deviation, etc.) might be, we can use traditional hypothesis testing and confidence intervals to express the reliability of our estimates. However, these approaches often rely too heavily on a specific hypothesis (for example, the null hypothesis). To move beyond this limitation, we introduce an approach that uses Bayes’ theorem to handle two competing hypotheses simultaneously. Using a prior distribution, which represents the information we have before observing the data, and a likelihood, which describes the distribution that the data are assumed to follow, we obtain a posterior distribution, representing our updated belief after observing the data. Based on this, we introduce the concepts of credible sets and Bayesian inference. Since obtaining the posterior distribution is often difficult, we will also discuss MCMC (Markov Chain Monte Carlo), an algorithm that helps approximate it. Using exam score data as an example, the seminar will present a new way of making statistical inferences. prerequisite_en: "None" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://youtu.be/0Po3OXIluHg" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2025Fall/thumbnails/14_Alternative Way to Estimate Our Test Rank, Bayesian Statistics.jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Alternative Way to Estimate Our Test Rank, Bayesian Statistics" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Alternative Way to Estimate Our Test Rank, Bayesian Statistics" url: "/seminars/2025Fall/slidedecks/14_Alternative Way to Estimate Our Test Rank, Bayesian Statistics.pdf" ---
