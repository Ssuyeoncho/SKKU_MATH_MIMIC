One simple approach is to try attaching copies of the polygon in different places. Here, “attaching them in different places” can also be viewed as moving the polygon using transformations such as translations. This perspective helps us understand the tessellation problem on the hyperbolic plane H2, which is otherwise difficult to visualize. In other words, the question “Which polygons tile H2?” can be transformed into an algebraic question about the existence of certain groups. The Poincaré Polygon Theorem provides one concrete answer to this question. This talk begins with the concept of H2, moves through its mathematical formalization, and then discusses the specific content of the Poincaré Polygon Theorem.
