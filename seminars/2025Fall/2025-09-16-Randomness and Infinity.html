At the same time, it is also a problematic and potentially dangerous concept that has caused various difficulties throughout the history of mathematics, such as in Zeno’s paradoxes. When infinity is connected with the concept of randomness, phenomena that differ from those in finite settings likewise arise. Methods of solving problems that appear ordinary may lead to inconsistencies in results, and events that should occur and those that should not may become intertwined. In this talk, we will examine the strange and interesting aspects of randomness—especially randomness and probability related to infinity—and also address common misunderstandings surrounding related problems. This talk begins with the concept of H2, moves through its mathematical formalization, and then discusses the specific content of the Poincaré Polygon Theorem.
