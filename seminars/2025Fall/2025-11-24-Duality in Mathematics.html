Try looking at it from the opposite direction.\"" abstract_body_en: | In everyday life, we often encounter pairs of concepts that cannot be separated from one another, such as “inside” and “outside,” “open” and “closed,” or “left” and “right.” Similar relationships also appear in mathematics, where they are known as duality. In this seminar, we will explore examples of duality that arise in various areas of mathematics, including set theory, Topology, and Linear Algebra. Through these examples, we will discuss the advantages of considering the opposite or dual perspective. Furthermore, we will examine how this idea—appearing across many branches of mathematics—can be expressed in the language of Category Theory. In addition, we will also discuss forms of duality that arise specifically within category theory itself. prerequisite_en: "Linear Algebra I, Topology I, and Abstract Algebra I (Abstract Algebra I will be mentioned, but it is not essential)" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://youtu.be/_aQ0aYnbJxY" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2025Fall/thumbnails/16_Duality in Mathematics.jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Duality in Mathematics" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Duality in Mathematics" url: "/seminars/2025Fall/slidedecks/16_Duality in Mathematics.pdf" ---
