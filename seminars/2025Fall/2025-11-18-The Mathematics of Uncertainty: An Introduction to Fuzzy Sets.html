Concepts such as “hot,” “close,” and “safe” are difficult to describe using the traditional binary distinction of true and false. For example, can we really say that 30°C is hot but 29°C is not? In 1965, Lotfi A. Zadeh proposed fuzzy sets as a way to mathematically handle such vagueness. By defining degrees between truth and falsity, this approach allows us to quantify the ambiguity that appears in real-world situations. In this seminar, we will first examine the core concepts of fuzzy sets and then show how fuzzy sets can be justified within the classical axiomatic system of set theory, Zermelo–Fraenkel set theory with the axiom of choice. We will discuss membership functions and fuzzy operations, introducing t-norms and t-conorms, which generalize the notions of intersection and union.
