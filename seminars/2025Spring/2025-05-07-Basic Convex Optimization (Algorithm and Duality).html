Moreover, in many cases, general optimization problems can be reformulated to be equivalent to convex optimization problems. Therefore, understanding convexity and convex optimization is a fundamental starting point for studying optimization theory. The overall goal of this seminar is to introduce the basic concepts of convex optimization. The main topics will include an introductory overview of first-order methods, as well as a brief discussion of Lagrangian duality. In particular, we will examine the convergence of several algorithms that work well in unconstrained settings, such as Gradient Descent, Stochastic Gradient Descent, and the Proximal Point Method. Finally, we will also provide a brief introduction to the dual problem. You have probably heard the term “Gradient Descent” at least once. While it may intuitively seem like an algorithm that should work well, proving its convergence is a completely different matter. In this seminar, we aim to go beyond that vague intuition and explore, in a mathematically precise way, how and why the algorithm converges, while sharing the intellectual enjoyment of the process. Although some prior knowledge (linear algebra and analysis) is mentioned, the material will not be overly difficult, so you are welcome to attend without concern. # prerequisite_label_en: "Prerequisite" # prerequisite_en: "Linear Algebra, Analysis" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://youtu.be/VxXUoJYWYvE" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2025Spring/thumbnails/11_Basic Convex Optimization (Algorithm and Duality).jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Basic Convex Optimization (Algorithm and Duality)" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Basic Convex Optimization (Algorithm and Duality)" url: "/seminars/2025Spring/slidedecks/11_Basic Convex Optimization (Algorithm and Duality).pdf" ---
