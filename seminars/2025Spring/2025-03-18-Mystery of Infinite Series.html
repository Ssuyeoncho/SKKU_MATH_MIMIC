While these can be quite perplexing, they are also deeply fascinating. I wanted many students—especially freshmen who have not yet been widely exposed to mathematics—to experience these kinds of ideas. I chose the topic of infinite series because it contains many counterintuitive phenomena, yet can be understood without requiring overly advanced mathematics. The main goal of this seminar is to introduce the Riemann rearrangement theorem, which shows that changing the order of summation in an infinite series can alter its value, and to convey the key ideas behind it at an intuitive level. In addition, I will briefly discuss what happens when the order of summation is changed in a less extreme way. The process of understanding the surprising fact that simply rearranging terms can change the result—through explicit calculations—will, I believe, be quite engaging.
