While doing so, we often wonder whether a given polynomial has rational roots. However, determining whether a polynomial has rational solutions is not an easy problem. To tackle this, I chose this topic to introduce a new number system—the p-adic numbers—and to present the Hasse–Minkowski Theorem, which provides equivalent conditions for this problem for certain polynomials. On the first day, I will discuss why we are led to introduce a new number system in order to determine the existence of solutions to equations. (When trying to find solutions in Z[x], two natural questions arise.) From there, I will introduce p-adic numbers as a way to approach solutions in Q[x] and explore their structure. On the second day, building on the p-adic numbers covered previously, I will introduce the Hasse–Minkowski Theorem—a local-global principle for quadratic forms—using tools such as the Hilbert symbol, along with several examples. It is fascinating that we can do analysis and algebra over number systems other than the real numbers, and that certain problems can become much simpler when translated into a different framework. Additionally, this seminar will provide insight into what kinds of approaches we can take when working with quadratic equations with rational coefficients. The material in this seminar will also serve as a foundational example for studying more advanced topics later on, such as local fields and class field theory.
