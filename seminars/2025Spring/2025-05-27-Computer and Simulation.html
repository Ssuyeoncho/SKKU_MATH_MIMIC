For example, when you check exam or assignment scores, you are usually given information such as your own score, the median (or average), the maximum, and the minimum. However, information about variance—which would allow you to estimate your rank—is often missing. This led me to choose this topic, as I wanted to explore how we can estimate values like variance in such situations and how to design simulations to make those estimations. Furthermore, I aim to go beyond theory and introduce efficient ways to compute such information using computers. While many people have experienced coding through DS (Data Science) courses, relatively few are familiar with how to evaluate the efficiency of the code they write. In this seminar, I will first explain the basic concepts of program efficiency and walk through examples to build understanding. I will also introduce the Monte Carlo Method, which estimates outcomes by sampling when information about the entire population is available, and the Bootstrap Method, which is used when such information is not available—both illustrated through practical examples. One of the strengths of simulation is that it can be easily applied to many simple situations we encounter in everyday life. It is particularly interesting that even when information about the entire population is limited or unavailable, we can still estimate new values, such as variance, based on given samples. Additionally, it is fascinating that even though simulations do not reflect actual reality, the information derived from them can still be highly reliable. I hope you won’t be discouraged by terms like statistics or coding. I’ve prepared this seminar to be as accessible and easy to understand as possible, and I hope it will be useful for estimating your grades in the future. # prerequisite_label_en: "Prerequisite" # prerequisite_en: "None" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://youtu.be/VTB0kuLFG4I" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2025Spring/thumbnails/16_Computer and Simulation.jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Computer and Simulation" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Computer and Simulation" url: "/seminars/2025Spring/slidedecks/16_Computer and Simulation.pdf" ---
