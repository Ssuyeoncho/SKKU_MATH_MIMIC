Since it was a function that defied intuition, I found it extremely fascinating, though I could not fully understand it at the time. I believe that many first- and second-year students, like myself back then, may feel the same way. For this reason, I chose this topic because exploring such unfamiliar “pathological” functions can offer a refreshing sense of mathematical enjoyment, especially for students who may feel exhausted after midterms. In this seminar, we will use basic tools from analysis to prove the differentiability of the Weierstrass function. Through this process, I hope that fellow undergraduate students will broaden their perspective on functions and experience the intellectual pleasure that mathematics can offer. I believe you will find it particularly interesting that, even when a function is defined as an infinite sum, we can still rigorously discuss its continuity and differentiability. Moreover, the process of understanding such properties introduces new ideas and ways of thinking, allowing for a deeper sense of intellectual enjoyment. Whether you want to impress your friends, explore unusual and intriguing functions, or are simply a first- or second-year student who has just finished midterms, this seminar has been prepared to be as accessible as possible.
