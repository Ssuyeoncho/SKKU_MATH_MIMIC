In such settings, it is crucial to detect and correct errors that inevitably occur during communication. In this seminar, I would like to introduce what polar codes are and how they work as a solution to this problem. In addition, reinforcement learning is widely used across many fields today, and I will demonstrate one of its methods—SARSA—through concrete examples. First, I will explain polar codes and aim to show how to determine important elements within them, such as frozen bits and information bits, using reinforcement learning and machine learning techniques. Along the way, I also plan to introduce some extended topics related to the seminar. Whenever we send photos or make phone calls, unintended noise can occur during transmission. One of the interesting aspects of this seminar is that you will gain a concrete understanding of how we currently solve these problems. Since signal error correction may be a relatively unfamiliar topic and the content can be quite dense, it might seem challenging at first.
