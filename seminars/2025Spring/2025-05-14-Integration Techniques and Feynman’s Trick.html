I chose this topic because it is fascinating that, without relying on advanced topics like complex analysis or Fourier analysis, one can evaluate seemingly complicated integrals using only high school or undergraduate-level calculus through Feynman’s technique. In this seminar, I will discuss the limitations of standard methods such as basic integration formulas, substitution, and integration by parts. I will then introduce Leibniz’s rule for differentiation under the integral sign, as well as Feynman’s technique. Through these ideas, I hope to present a new perspective on evaluating integrals and demonstrate that even complicated-looking functions can be handled with these methods.
