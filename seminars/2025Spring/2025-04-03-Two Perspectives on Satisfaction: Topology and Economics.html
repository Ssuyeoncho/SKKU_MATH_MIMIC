The topic of this seminar, Sperner’s Lemma, is a theorem that can be proven in two different ways—both physically and mathematically. By following the proof process, I hope to share with you the joy and excitement that comes from moments of realization. Furthermore, I aim to explore the concept of “satisfaction,” which may seem far removed from topology, and use it to explain a fundamental human desire. However, since approaching “satisfaction” purely from a mathematical perspective has its limitations, I chose this topic in order to complement it with an economic perspective and demonstrate the connection between these two fields. Personally, I have a strong interest in both economics and topology. In this seminar, I would like to present the proofs of Sperner’s Lemma—one from a physical perspective and the other from a mathematical perspective—and then connect these ideas to economics. Through this process, I hope to demonstrate how different academic disciplines can be meaningfully integrated. I also hope that you will come to appreciate the appeal of these two fields that I deeply enjoy. I believe it will be particularly interesting to explore the fundamental human desire for “satisfaction” through both mathematical and economic lenses. By introducing how Sperner’s Lemma relates to this idea, along with the economic perspective, you will be able to discover the intersection between the two disciplines in an engaging way. Although topology and economics may sound like intimidating subjects, I have prepared this seminar so that anyone can understand it.
