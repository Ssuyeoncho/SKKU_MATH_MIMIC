In addition, I would like to discuss how the Euler–Lagrange equation, a key tool in the calculus of variations, can be applied to other problems. The goal of this seminar is to explain what the Euler–Lagrange equation is—one of the central ideas in the calculus of variations—and how to apply it to solve problems. Through this seminar, you will learn how Newton was able to solve the brachistochrone problem proposed by Bernoulli in just one day. You will also gain a rigorous understanding of why the shortest path between two points is a straight line.
