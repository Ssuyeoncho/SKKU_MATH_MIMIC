As a result, many students find combinatorics unfamiliar when they first encounter it in university. However, as seen in the case of June Huh, who was awarded the Fields Medal for his work connecting combinatorics and algebraic geometry, the influence of combinatorics in modern mathematics is significant. For this reason, I chose this topic to introduce my field of study, combinatorics. I hope to give you a sense of what combinatorics studies, what kinds of objects it finds interesting, and how it influences mathematics, so that whether you study discrete mathematics or combinatorics and graph theory in the future, you will feel a sense of familiarity and interest when you encounter it. What I find most appealing about combinatorics is its ability to transform difficult mathematics into something intuitive and accessible. I have prepared this seminar so that you can experience this appeal indirectly through examples. Although combinatorics is a field that is closest to human intuition, it remains unfamiliar to many people. In this seminar, I will introduce combinatorics through Catalan numbers and demonstrate how problems that appear difficult can be approached and simplified using combinatorial methods.
