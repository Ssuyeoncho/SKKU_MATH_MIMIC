But is it really possible to determine whether every statement is true or false? The natural response is to assume that it is. However, Gödel provided a fascinating answer to this question through his groundbreaking ideas. Through this seminar, I hope you will come to appreciate the greatness of mathematics, which is why I chose this topic. This seminar begins with Cantor’s set theory, moves through Russell’s paradox, and culminates in Gödel’s incompleteness theorems. By following this flow of mathematical debates, participants will gain insight into how mathematics has developed over time. Ultimately, the goal is to create a space where we can deeply appreciate the beauty and greatness of mathematics and share that sense of wonder together. First, by tracing the discussion from set theory to questions about the completeness of mathematics, you will have an opportunity to reflect on what mathematics truly is and reconsider its fundamental nature. Second, through Gödel’s incompleteness theorems, we can question whether mathematics is indeed a perfect discipline. Not only the conclusion of these theorems, but also the methods of proof themselves are fascinating, offering moments of shock and intellectual excitement. Is the statement “mathematics is perfect” true or false? Since this seminar happens to take place on April 1st—April Fools’ Day—no matter what answer you come up with, you might just be told it’s false!
