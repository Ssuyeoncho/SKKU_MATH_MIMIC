Ian Macdonald\"" abstract_body_en: | I usually enjoy abstract strategy games. While playing a subway management game called Mini Metro, I started wondering what would happen if I approached the process of creating and analyzing my own strategies for achieving high scores from a mathematical perspective. Using in-game data, I tried building my own formulas and incorporating concepts from discrete mathematics that I learned in my first year. Through this kind of direct modeling, I was actually able to achieve better results. This experience led me to choose this topic, as I wanted to share the enjoyment of mathematically modeling phenomena that occur in games and everyday life. In this seminar, I will first give a brief introduction to Mini Metro and show some live gameplay. Next, I will compare my strategy with other alternatives and explain what makes it effective. After that, I will assume certain special conditions and attempt to generalize the strategy. If possible, there will also be time for participants to try playing themselves and think about their own algorithms. In any game, intuitive strategies tend to emerge once you start playing. However, in practice, those strategies often turn out to be inefficient. Experiencing that gap firsthand through this seminar should be quite interesting. Moreover, by going beyond intuition and applying mathematical analysis, you can operate the game far more efficiently—highlighting the precision and power of mathematical modeling.
