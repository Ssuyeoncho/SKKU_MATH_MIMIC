The main program of the booth was "Divide pi into Three Equal Parts," planned in celebration of Pi Day on March 14. Participants chose either an Oh Yes or a Big Pie, divided it into three pieces themselves, and then competed by measuring the weights of the three pieces and comparing the standard deviation. As a hands-on event built around a mathematical idea, the program drew strong interest, with a total of 190 students participating over the two days. Some students even ranked highly through especially inventive ways of trisecting the pie. MIMIC also prepared cast puzzles at the booth so that visitors could enjoy a lighter mathematical activity alongside the main event. The booth further included an introduction to the club, helping students get to know MIMIC's activities and atmosphere in a more comfortable way. Supported by the active promotion carried out both through the booth and at the freshman orientation program, MIMIC grew to more than 150 members. In the process, not only students from the Department of Mathematics but also students from other departments who were interested in mathematics, as well as mathematics students from other universities, newly joined the club.
