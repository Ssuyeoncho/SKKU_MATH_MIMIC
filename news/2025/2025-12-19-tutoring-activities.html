This semester, tutoring sessions were held on a regular basis for foundational science liberal arts courses such as Calculus 2, Discrete Mathematics, and Linear Algebra, providing steady academic support throughout the term. MIMIC's tutoring is meaningful not simply because it helps improve grades, but because senior students in the Department of Mathematics support learning in a way that helps junior students understand the connections between major courses and build a solid academic foundation. By looking together at how current material leads into later coursework, the program aimed to help students continue their major studies more steadily. In addition, MIMIC operated a KakaoTalk open chat called the "MIMIC BSM Q&A Chat," a question channel for foundational science liberal arts courses, so that students could still ask questions and receive help even if they did not participate directly in tutoring. A total of 22 senior and junior students joined the chat, and questions and answers related to Calculus, Discrete Mathematics, Linear Algebra, Probability and Statistics, and other subjects were actively exchanged. More broadly, the club's regular communication channel also fostered a culture in which questions about mathematics were met with responses from both senior and junior members, making ongoing academic support possible throughout the semester. Tutoring teams formed within MIMIC also took part in the Sungkyun Tutoring Program and received several recognitions, including Best Activity Team (1st place), Excellent Activity Team (2nd place), and Excellent Tutor Award (2nd place). These results show that the tutoring activities MIMIC has continued within the club were also well recognized beyond the club itself.
