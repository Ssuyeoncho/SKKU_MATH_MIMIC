This semester, tutoring sessions were held on a regular basis for foundational science liberal arts courses such as Calculus 2, Discrete Mathematics, and Linear Algebra, providing steady academic support throughout the term. MIMIC's tutoring is meaningful not simply because it helps improve grades, but because senior students in the Department of Mathematics support learning in a way that helps participants understand the connections between subjects in the major and study mathematical ideas more deeply. By looking together at how current material leads into later coursework, the program aims to help students build a more stable foundation for their major studies. In addition, MIMIC operated question channels for foundational science liberal arts courses and a club communication channel so that students could freely ask questions related to Calculus, Discrete Mathematics, Linear Algebra, Probability and Statistics, and other subjects even outside tutoring hours, and receive guidance from senior students. Tutoring teams formed within MIMIC also took part in the Sungkyun Tutoring Program and received several recognitions, including Best Activity Team (1st place), Excellent Activity Team (2nd place), and Excellent Tutor Award (2nd place). These results reflect the strong evaluation of the tutoring activities that MIMIC has continued within the club.
